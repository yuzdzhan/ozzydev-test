About OzzyDev

A field journal from the place where web development meets artificial intelligence.

OzzyDev exists for one kind of person: the builder who is equally excited about a clean component tree and a well-tuned prompt. We believe the next decade of the web will be written by developers who treat AI as a first-class part of their toolbox — not a gimmick, and not a threat.

Every post here is designed to be useful by the end of your coffee break. We favor working code over hot takes, and we ship examples you can copy, paste, and actually run.

Our take on AI-assisted writing

We're transparent about it: many posts on this site are drafted with the help of large language models, then edited, fact-checked, and tested by a human before publishing. We think that's exactly how AI should be used — as a fast first draft and a tireless research assistant, with a person in the loop for judgment, accuracy, and taste.

What you'll find here

  • Applied AI — RAG, agents, embeddings, and prompt engineering for real apps.
  • Frontend craft — modern JavaScript, CSS, and framework deep dives.
  • Dev tooling — the editors, CLIs, and AI copilots that make us faster.
  • Shipping — deploying and running web apps without losing sleep.

Who's behind it

OzzyDev is an independent publication run by a small crew of web developers who got a little too curious about machine learning. We're not affiliated with any framework vendor or model provider — we just write about the tools we actually use.

"The best way to predict the future is to build it — and then write a tutorial so everyone else can too."
